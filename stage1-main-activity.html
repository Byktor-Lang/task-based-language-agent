La familia — Main TBLT Activity
Task-Based Language Teaching  |  9th Grade Spanish  |  Novice-Mid
Grammar: Present tense of ser • Definite articles (el, la, los, las) Time: ~40 min Format: Information-gap (pair)
A   B
Driving Problem
Your partner has a partial family tree with nodes only you can identify — and you have missing nodes only they can identify. Neither of you can finish your tree alone. Ask and answer questions entirely in Spanish to build a complete picture of the Vargas family together.
Language rule: All questions and answers must be in Spanish. If you forget a word, describe it in Spanish or ask “¿Cómo se dice …?” — do not switch to English.

Paso 2 El árbol genealógico — Complete the Family Tree ~18 min  |  pair — DO NOT show your tree to your partner

This is the main task. You and your partner each have a partial tree of the same fictional family — the Vargas family. Your two trees have different nodes missing (dashed red border). Ask questions in Spanish to find out who is missing from your tree. Write the person’s name and their relationship word in each blank node.

Question & Answer Frames — use ser in every sentence
  • ¿Quién es el/la          de         ?
  • ¿Cómo se llama el/la          de         ?
  •   es el/la          de         .
  • ¿Son los/las          de         ? → Sí, son los/las  . / No, son los/las  .

Paso 3 ¿Coincidimos? — Cross-Check Your Trees ~7 min  |  pair

Now you may look at each other’s trees. Compare them node by node. If any name or relationship label differs, use the repair frames below to resolve it in Spanish.

Repair Frames

  • No,   es el/la  , no el/la  .
  • ¿Quién es el/la   de  ?

Paso 4 Uno o muchos — Singular or Plural? ~5 min  |  pair

Your partner points to one node or a group of nodes on the completed tree. Say a full sentence using ser, the correct family noun in singular or plural form, and the correct definite article (el / la / los / las). Take turns: Partner A points first, Partner B answers, then switch.

Paso 5 ¿Y en tu familia? — Synthesis ~5 min  |  pair

Use the same language to talk about your own real or imagined family. Answer your partner’s questions about two people in your family using complete sentences with ser.

Synthesis Frames
  • ¿Tienes hermanos? → Sí, tengo [número] hermano/s. Se llama/n  . / No, soy hijo/a único/a.
  • ¿Cómo se llama tu  ? → Mi   se llama  .
  • ¿Quién es   en tu familia? → Es mi  .
  • ¿Tienes primos/as? ¿Cuántos?

Success Criteria — What you are aiming for today
  • I correctly identified at least 3 relationships my partner described (listening comprehension).
  • I used both singular and plural forms of at least one family noun correctly (e.g., hermano / hermanos; hijo / hijos).
  • I completed the task goal — filled in all missing nodes — using only Spanish.

Why this matters: Being able to identify and state who people are in relation to each other lets you participate in basic personal conversations in a Spanish-speaking context — a skill you will use any time you meet someone new.
