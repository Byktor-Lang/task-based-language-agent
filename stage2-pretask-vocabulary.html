La familia — Pre-Task Vocabulary Introduction
Task-Based Language Teaching  |  9th Grade Spanish  |  Novice-Mid
Grammar focus: Present tense of ser • Definite articles (el, la, los, las) Sequence: SORT → CLOZE → MATCH Time: ~20 min Stage: 2 of 3 (before the main activity)
Purpose of this activity
You are about to do an information-gap speaking task about a family tree — entirely in Spanish. These three exercises introduce the vocabulary and grammar structures you will need. Complete them before the main task begins.

Ejercicio 1 SORT — Classify by Article & Gender ~6 min  |  individual

Each Spanish family noun uses a specific definite article that shows its gender and number. Sort all 12 vocabulary items from the reference strip into the correct category below. Write the full term with its article (e.g., el padre, la madre). Some items will appear only in one box; items with both forms (e.g., el abuelo / la abuela) go in both the masculine AND feminine boxes.

el — Masculine singular
e.g. el padre
la — Feminine singular
e.g. la madre
los — Masculine plural
e.g. los hijos, los padres
las — Feminine plural
(are any of the 12 items feminine plural by default?)

Notice: Spanish nouns ending in -o are usually masculine (el hermano), ending in -a are usually feminine (la hermana). The plural los is used for a mixed-gender group: los padres means “parents” (father AND mother); los hijos means “children” (sons and/or daughters).

Answer Key (Teacher)

el (masculine singular): el abuelo, el hermano, el hijo, el padre, el medio hermano, el primo, el sobrino, el tío
la (feminine singular): la abuela, la hermana, la hija, la madre, la media hermana, la prima, la sobrina, la tía
los (masculine/mixed plural): los hijos, los padres, los parientes, los primos [when mixed-gender group; los abuelos for grandparents as a pair]
las (feminine plural): None of the 12 PVS items have a fixed feminine-plural form as their canonical entry — accept student explanations noting that “las hijas,” “las primas,” etc. are possible but not listed as standalone PVS items. Award credit for correct reasoning.

Connection to the main task: In the family-tree activity, every question and answer requires the right article. Sorting now means you will not hesitate when you say “Es el primo de Marco” or “Son los hijos de Carmen.”
Ejercicio 2 CLOZE — Complete with ser and the Correct Family Term ~7 min  |  individual, then check with a partner

Fill in each blank with (a) the correct form of ser and (b) the correct family noun from the vocabulary bank below. Use the article that matches the gender and number of the person(s) named. The first item is done as an example.

Word Bank — choose the correct form for each blank
  • ser forms: es (he/she/it is)  •  son (they are)
    Family nouns: el/la abuelo/a • el/la hermano/a • el/la hijo/a • los hijos • la madre • el/la medio/a hermano/a • el padre • los padres • los parientes • el/la primo/a • el/la sobrino/a • el/la tío/a
0. Carmen es la madre de Sofía.  (Example — done for you)
1. Roberto     de Carmen.  (Roberto is Carmen’s father’s father)
2. Sofía y Marco     de Carmen.  (children of Carmen)
3. Luis   el   de Marco.  (the brother of Marco’s father)
4. Elena   la   de Sofía.  (Elena is Luis’s daughter; Sofía is the child of Luis’s sibling)
5. Lucía   la   de Marco.  (Lucía is Sofía’s daughter; Marco is Sofía’s brother)
6. Diego   el   de Sofía.  (Diego shares only one parent with Sofía)
7. Carmen y David     de Sofía y Marco.  (their parents, as a pair)
8. Roberto, Rosa, Luis, Ana, y los demás familiares     de Sofía.  (all the extended family members)
Answer Key (Teacher)

1. es / el abuelo    2. son / los hijos    3. es / tío    4. es / prima    5. es / sobrina    6. es / medio hermano    7. son / los padres    8. son / los parientes

Connection to the main task: Every sentence you just completed mirrors the exact frames you will use in the family-tree task: “[Name] es el/la [family word] de [Name].” The verb form (es vs. son) depends on whether you are talking about one person or more than one — the same choice you will make dozens of times during the activity.

Connection to the main task: During the family-tree activity, your partner will describe a missing node using exactly this kind of relationship reasoning — “It’s the child of Luis” or “She shares only one parent with Sofía.” Matching now trains you to map those descriptions onto the correct Spanish term instantly.
Ready for the main task? Before your teacher signals the transition, check:
  • I can say es (one person) vs. son (more than one) without hesitating.
  • I know which article (el / la / los) goes with at least 8 of the 12 family terms.
  • I can describe what a primo/a, sobrino/a, and medio hermano/a are in English (so I can understand my partner’s description in Spanish).
