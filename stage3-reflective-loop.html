La familia — Reflective Loop
Task-Based Language Teaching  |  9th Grade Spanish  |  Novice-Mid  |  Post-Task Written Expansion
Grammar: Present tense of ser • Definite articles (el, la, los, las) Register: Formal description (audience: teacher / school staff) Time: ~25 min Stage: 3 of 3 (after the main activity)
What this activity is for
You just completed a speaking task in which you identified family relationships in Spanish in order to participate in a basic personal conversation — a skill you will use whenever you meet someone new in a Spanish-speaking context. This written activity extends that skill: you will take what you said out loud during the family-tree task and transform it into a short, formal written description that an adult could read. Moving from spoken to written Spanish in a formal register is a real-world transfer of exactly the language you just practiced.
Complication to address in your writing
During the family-tree task, you discovered that Diego Torres is el medio hermano of Sofía Vargas — they share only one parent, not two. If someone reads a description of this family and sees only “hermano,” they will get the wrong picture. Your written description must make this distinction clear so that a reader who was not in the room understands the exact relationship.
Task outcome
Both partners agreed during the cross-check that Diego Torres is correctly labeled el medio hermano of Sofía, not simply el hermano. Your written description should reflect that agreed-upon outcome and use ser to state it with the appropriate definite article.

Parte 2 Escenario y descripción formal — Writing Task ~12 min  |  individual
Scenario
A new school counselor at a bilingual school is preparing a file on the Vargas family. She has asked for a brief written description of who each family member is and how they are related to each other. She has specifically asked that the description clarify whether any siblings share both parents or only one parent — because this affects certain forms she needs to complete.

Write a formal description of the Vargas family for this counselor. Address her as Estimada señora consejera at the beginning. Write at least 6 complete sentences in Spanish. Use ser in every sentence. Include the definite article (el, la, los, las) before every family noun.
Phrase Bank — formal written register (use these as models)
  • • Estimada señora consejera:    Atentamente, [your name] (for opening/closing)
  • • [Full name] es el/la [family noun] de [full name].
  • • [Full name 1] y [full name 2] son los/las [family noun] de [full name 3].
  • • Es importante señalar que [full name] es el medio hermano de [full name], no el hermano, porque comparten solo un padre.
  • • Los parientes de la familia Vargas son: [list].

Parte 3 Revisión — Self-Assessment Checklist ~6 min  |  individual

Read your description once more. Check each box only when you are confident the statement is true for your writing. If you cannot check a box, revise that part before submitting.

  • Transfer goal: My description shows that I can identify and state who people are in relation to each other — the skill I practiced speaking and now demonstrate in writing for a real audience.
  • Listening comprehension verified: I correctly identified at least 3 relationships from the spoken task (my description reflects what my partner and I agreed on during cross-check).
  • Singular and plural forms: I used both the singular (el/la [noun]) and the plural (los/las [noun]) of at least one family word correctly in my description.
  • Task goal reflected: My description accounts for every person in the completed family tree — no node is left unnamed or unplaced.
  • Complication addressed: My description clearly states that Diego Torres is el medio hermano of Sofía Vargas and explains (or implies) that they share only one parent.
  • Formal register: I used full names (first and last) for every person. I have no one-word or abbreviated answers. My sentences are complete.
  • Grammar accuracy: Every sentence uses es or son (not another form of ser). The definite article (el, la, los, las) before each family noun matches the noun’s gender and number.
  • Spanish only: My entire description is in Spanish. I did not use English words.
